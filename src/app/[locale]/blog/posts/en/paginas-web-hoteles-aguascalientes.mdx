## The Real Problem: Commissions vs. Full Control

Many hotels in Aguascalientes fall into the trap of "being" on the internet only through OTAs. While they provide visibility, they take away your control.

  * **You Lose the Customer Relationship:** You don't get your guests' data for future marketing campaigns.
  * **Price Wars:** You compete directly on price in a list alongside dozens of other hotels.
  * **Reduced Margins:** Every booking costs you money.

Your own website gives you back control, allows you to build your brand, and, most importantly, **every booking is 100% yours**.

## Essential Features for Hotel Websites

When you start to **research brands or services** for your website design, don't just get carried away by visuals. A successful hotel website requires specific technology.

### An Integrated and Efficient Booking Engine

This is the heart of your site. It must be:

  * **Intuitive:** The customer should be able to book in 3 steps or less.
  * **Secure:** With reliable payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, Conekta).
  * **Synchronized:** Ideally, it should connect to your Channel Manager to avoid overbooking with OTAs.

### Responsive and Mobile-Optimized Design (Mobile-First)

According to industry statistics, [more than 60% of hotel bookings](https://www.google.com/search?q=https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/consumer-insights/consumer-trends/mobile-hotel-booking-trends/) start on a mobile device. If your site doesn't look perfect and work fast on a smartphone, you are losing customers searching for "hotel near San Marcos Fair" from their phone.

### Local SEO: Show Up When They Search for You

Your site must be optimized for Google to show you when someone searches:

  * "Hotels in downtown Aguascalientes"
  * "Hotel near Isla San Marcos"
  * "Business hotel Aguascalientes"

This is achieved with a robust local SEO strategy, something a simple website builder won't offer.

### Professional Photo and Video Gallery

Guests buy with their eyes. Your site must display high-quality images of your rooms, amenities, and common areas. Dark or low-resolution photos create immediate distrust.

## Researching Brands and Services: What to Look for in a Web Developer?

The market is full of options, from "nephews" who make websites to expensive digital agencies. When researching, focus on these three pillars:

#### 1. Specific Hospitality Portfolio

Have they worked with hotels before? Understanding the hotel industry is crucial. They will know the importance of a booking engine and a user experience (UX) focused on conversion.

#### 2. Focus on Conversion (CRO)

You don't want a "pretty" website; you want a website that **sells**. The developer should talk to you about "sales funnels," "conversion rates," and "calls to action" (CTAs).

#### 3. Support and Modern Technology

Your site must be fast, secure (with an SSL certificate), and easy for you to self-manage to change rates or photos.
